01 / The Eduba frame

Editorial-ops and recruiter-workflow compound faster than matching engines.

We work on a simple idea. About 60% of what a marketplace plus media business does every day is traditional code and database work. About 30% is rule-based logic. Around 10% is a genuine AI problem. The job is sorting those three into the right layers before anything is built, and routing the compounding work to the layer where it pays off.

Applied to the ClearanceJobs stack, the map resolves quickly. Candidate data, employer subscriptions, AgileATS reconciliation, and the compensation-data moat are code-and-database work. Editorial SME assignment, recruiter SLAs, and candidate-consent flows are rule-based logic. The genuinely AI surface is narrower than the market hype: it is matching-quality evaluation, editorial-ops assist, and recruiter-assist agents on top of the existing engine. Those are the investments that compound inside a public-company procurement cycle.

That is the work we would propose to discuss with the ClearanceJobs leadership team and the ClearanceJobs News editorial bench.
